Introduction

The Arris SURFboard SBG6700-AC wireless gateway is the premier wireless cable modem and router on the market. The SBG6700-AC provides ultra high-speed wired or wireless Internet access to your computer and other wireless network devices on your home or small business network.

The SBG6700-AC connects to the existing coaxial cable connection in your home and does not require any new wiring. This quick start guide will help you install your SBG6700-AC and set up a secure wireless network connection on your home or small business network. To take advantage of the 802.11ac wireless performance or beam forming capabilities, your wireless network must also support 802.11ac.

In the Box

Everything you need to get started can be found in the Arris SURFboard 6700-AC box. During the installation you may have to contact your service provider for assistance. To save time, make sure you have the following information nearby. The information is found on the bottom of the modem:

  • Gateway model name (SBG6700-AC)
  • HFC MAC ID
  • Serial Number (S/N)

    • Install the Gateway

    Connect the SBG6700-AC to the existing Coaxial Cable Port in your home or small business. Place your SBG6700- AC above ground level and away from devices that cause network interference.

    1. Connect the Coaxial Cable from the cable wall outlet or RF splitter (optional) to the Cable connector on the rear of your SBG6700-AC. Hand tighten each connector.
    2. Connect the Ethernet Cable to the Ethernet port on your computer and to an Ethernet port on the rear of your SBG6700-AC.
    3. Connect the power cord to the Power port on the rear of your SBG6700-AC and then plug it into an electrical wall outlet.

    This automatically powers On your SBG6700-AC. Check the front panel LEDs on your SBG6700-AC. During the initial startup, the Power, Receive, Send, and Online LEDs on the front of the modem will Flash. Afterwards, check that all four LEDs are lit solid green or blue.

    Test the Gateway Connection

    Your computer automatically accesses the Internet after the gateway installation. You should still perform a gateway connection test to ensure that there are no problems.

    1. Power ON your computer and log in.
    2. Contact your wireless service provider to activate your SBG6700-AC.
    3. Open a web browser on your computer, such as Internet Explorer, Google Chrome, Firefox, or Safari.
    4. In the Address bar, type a valid URL (such as www.surfboard.com) and press Enter.

    The Arris website should open.

    If the website fails to open, unplug the gateway and plug it back in after 30 seconds. Contact your wireless service provider if this does not work.

    Connect From Your Computer

    You can connect to the SBG6700-AC modem from your computer's network settings. The SSID (network name) and password can be found in the quick start guide.

    The steps for setting up a wireless network may differ slightly depending on the operating system installed on your computer. The following steps apply to OS X El Capitan.

    1. Click System Preferences.
    2. Click Network.
    3. Click the Lock icon on the bottom left hand corner of the window to change your settings. If your computer has an administrator password you will have to type it.
    4. Click the Network Name drop-down menu.
    5. Select the SSID (network name) for your SBG6700-AC. The SSID (network name) can be found on the bottom of your SBG6700-AC.
    6. Type the preprogrammed password. The SBG6700-ACâ€™s password is on the bottom of the router.
    7. Click the Lock key in the lower left hand corner to save your changes.
    8. Test your wireless connection when done.

    Connect With the Wireless Pairing Button

    The Wireless Pairing Button is the easiest way to connect your desktop computer or laptop. The Wireless Pairing Button can be found on top of your SBG6700-AC wireless gateway. Make sure your laptop or PC is on and within range of the router.

    1. Power ON the SBG6700-AC and other WPS-enabled wireless devices to be connected to your wireless network.
    2. Press and Hold the WPS button located on the top of the SBG6700-AC.
    3. Press and Hold the WPS button on your desktop computer or laptop for five seconds, and then Release.
    4. Test your wireless connection when you are done.
    5. Repeat steps 2 and 3 for each additional device.

    Connect to Your Mobile Device or Tablet

    Connecting your mobile device or tablet to Wi-Fi network will allow your device to run web-based applications faster. The SBG6700-AC supports up to eight devices. Make sure you have the SSID (network name) and password nearby.

    The steps below differ slighly depending on your operating system. The steps below are for an iPhone 6 running iOS 10.2.1

    1. Open Settings.
    2. Click Wi-Fi.
    3. Turn Wi-Fi on.
    4. Select your network
    5. In the Password field, enter your password.
    6. Open a web page or web based application to test the connection.

    Test the Wireless Network Connection

    The following task determines if a wireless connection has been established between the SBG6700-AC and your wireless devices.

    1. If connected, disconnect the Ethernet cable from both your computer and the SBG6700-AC.
    2. Check the Wireless LED on the SBG6700-AC front panel is lit solid green.
    3. Open a web browser.
    4. In the Address bar, type a valid URL (such as www.surfboard.com) and then press Enter.

    If your device is connected to the Internet, the valid URL will load.

    Unplug your gateway for 30 seconds and plug it back in if the valid URL does not load. Use these troubleshooting tips or call your wireless service provider if the problem continues.

    Maximize Network Security

    The SBG6700-AC is one of the most secure modems on the market. In just a few extra minutes you can improve your network security. You can also use the parental controls to limit usage and accessible content on the modem. These features will allow you to take better control of your modem and provide additional network security. The SBG6700-AC has a wide variety of security features to ensure there is no unauthorized access to your wireless network.

    To prevent unauthorized access to your wireless network, you must immediately change the default user name and password after your first login to the wireless gateway settings.

    Follow these guidelines when creating a new password:

    1. Ensure your password cannot be guessed easily
    2. Use phrases instead of names if that is easier for you to remember
    3. Use a combination of lowercase letters, uppercase letters, numbers and symbols
    4. Do not reuse passwords across platforms
    5. Regularly change your password

    Change the Default SSID and Password

    The SBG6700-AC modem comes with a pre-programmed name and password. Keeping the default password can compromise your network security. For security reasons, it is recommended that you change the SSID (network name) and password after you complete the initial setup.

    For security reasons, it is recommended your password use letters, numbers and symbols. A strong password also uses uppercase and lowercase letters. There are no password requirements but a 10 character password with the above features will provide strong security to your home wireless network.

    1. Open a web browser on the computer connected to the SBG6700-AC.
    2. Type 192.168.0.1 in the Address bar. Press enter.
    3. Type admin in the Username field. Type password in the Password field. Click Login.
    4. Click the Wireless Settings drop-down menu.
    5. Click Primary Network Settings.
    6. In the Network Name (SSID) field, type the new name of your wireless network.
    7. In the Passphrase field, type your new password.
    8. Scroll to the bottom of the screen.
    9. Click Apply.

    Set Up Firewall Protection

    Configuring the firewall filters is an easy way to increase your network security. A firewall typically establishes a barrier between a trusted, secure internal network and another outside network. Firewalls monitor and control the incoming and outgoing traffic on your network based on predetermined security rules.


    1. Open a web browser on the computer connected to the SBG6700-AC.
    2. Type 192.168.0.1 in the Address bar. Press Enter.
    3. In the Username filed, type admin. In the Password field, type password.
    4. From the Firewall drop-down menu, click Protection Level.
    5. In the Firewall Selection Settings drop down menu, select your desired firewall protection level. Available firewall protection levels:

      6. Off - No security, highest risk

      Low - Common security, higher risk

      Medium - Safer configuration, modest risk

      High - Safest configuration, highest security

    6. Select your desired web filters.
    7. Click Apply.

    Set Up Parental Controls

    Parental controls are a great way to control the content that can be accessed from your wireless network. Parental controls can be set up to limit the usage and activity for specific users and devices on your wireless network. You can limit the activity based on time of day or days of the week.

    Parental controls can be useful if you wish to:

  • Allow or block certain Internet sites.
  • Allow or block access to specific web addresses.
  • Set time limitations for computers and Internet access.
  • Create specific login, timed access and content filtering rules.

    1. Open a web browser on the computer connected to the SBG6700-AC.
    2. Type 192.168.0.1 in the Address bar. Press Enter.
    3. Type admin in the Username field. Type password in the Password field.
    4. Click Login.
    5. In the Firewall drop down menu, click Parental Control.
    6. In the Current Time Zone drop down menu, select your timezone.
    7. Select Yes or No to enable Daylight Savings Time.
    8. Click Apply to set the timezone.
    9. Click Create to set up Parent Controls.
    10. In the Description field, create a name for your new user profile.
    11. In the MAC Address field, enter the Twelve digit MAC address for the device. Filters with no MAC address apply to all MAC addresses.
    12. Enter the web address of the Internet site that you want to block or access.
    13. In the Start Port field, enter the Starting Port Number.
    14. In the End Port field, enter the Ending Port Number. Filters with no ports entered apply to all ports. See Port Filtering if you need help setting up a port filter or applying ports.
    15. Select the days of the week that you want to allow the selected user to access the Internet.
    16. Select the time range that you want to allow the selected user to access the Internet.
    17. Select to Allow or Block Internet access for the times and days you set previously.
    18. Select On or Off in the Enabled field to enable or disable this Parental Control restriction.
    19. Click Apply.

    Set Up an IP Filter

    IP address filtering is a great way to block specific devices from your wireless network. IP address filtering is device specific. Each device has its own unique IP address.

    Before you begin, you will need to know the IP address of the device you want to block before you create a filter. The process involves creating IP filters containing the starting and ending IP address range you wish to block.

    For example, you can assign those PCs or devices static or reserved IP addresses such as 192.168.0.100 and 192.168.0.101. Then, you could create an IP filter Start Address at the beginning of the range (192.168.0.90) and an End Address at the end of the range (192.168.0.110). You could then enable the filter by checking the Enabled box and pressing Apply. Later, when any PC or device assigned a network IP address between 192.168.0.89 and 192.168.0.111 would try to access the Internet, that computer or device would be blocked by the IP address filter.

    1. Open a web browser on the computer connected to the SBG6700-AC.
    2. Type 192.168.0.1 in the Address bar and then press Enter.
    3. In the Username field, type admin. In the Password field, type password and then click Login.
    4. In the Advanced drop down menu, click IP Filtering.
    5. In the Start Address field, enter the Least Significant Byte of the Starting IP address. The least significant byte is the farthest number to the right in an IP address.
    6. In the End Address field, enter the Least Significant Byte of the Ending IP addressM This will ensure that all devices with IP addresses within the range will be blocked from your network.
    7. Select Enabled to activate the IP address filter.
    8. Click Apply.
    9. Repeat steps 5 through 7 for each additional range of IP addresses that you want to block Internet access.

    Set Up Port Filtering

    Ports are virtual pathways that the SBG6700-AC and other devices use for incoming and outgoing traffic. Port filtering can be used to define port filters that prevent all network devices from sending outgoing traffic to specific IP ports.

    Specifying a starting and ending port range means you determine what traffic is allowed on a per-port basis. The specified port ranges are blocked for all devices, and this setting is not IP address or MAC address specific. For example if you wanted to block all computers on your home network from accessing Internet content, you would create the port filter below. Blocking ports and filtering ports can improve your network security.

  • Set Start Port to 50
  • Set End Port to 50
  • Set Protocol to TCP
  • Select Enabled

    • Block a Single Port

    You may want to filter a single port to block services that would use that port. You can do this by creating a port filter with an identical Start Port and End Port, like 50. Then you select UPD, TCP, or Both for the affected protocol. The filter is enabled when you check the Enabled box and press Apply. As a result, all services trying to use the filtered port will be blocked.

    Block a Range of Ports

    You can also filter a range of ports to block services that would use that range of ports. For example, a Start Port filter at the beginning of the range (50) and an End Port filter at the end of the range (350). The filter is activated when you check the Enabled box and click Apply. As a result, all services trying to the filtered port range will be blocked.

    The same steps are used to block a single port or a range of ports. To create port filters:

    1. Open a web browser on the computer connected to the SBG6700-AC.
    2. Type 192.168.0.1 in the Address bar.
    3. In the Username field, type admin. In the Password field, type password and then click Login.
    4. Click the Advanced menu on the SBG6700-AC Manager main menu bar.
    5. Click Port Filtering in the Advanced sub-menu options.
    6. In the Start Port field, enter the Starting Port Number of the Port Filtering range.
    7. In the End Port field, enter the Ending Port Number of the Port Filtering range. Enter the same number as the Starting Port Number if you want to block a single port.
    8. In the Protocol drop down list, select TCP, UDP, or BOTH. Both is the default choice.
    9. Select Enabled to activate the selected IP port filters.
    10. Click Apply

    Manage Your Wireless Network

    The SBG6700-AC is easy to configure. Managing your wireless network is done by accessing the Wireless Gateway Manager. The SBG6700-AC offers many customizable features in addition to its numerous security features.

    Change the Wireless Channel

    Sometimes the best way to improve your wireless connection is to change the wireless channel. Network interference may occur at any time when using a wireless connection. This may be caused by wireless access points using the same channel as your SBG6700-AC. You can change the channel on your SBG6700-AC to improve network connectivity and prevent network interference. Channel 1 is the default channel on the SBG6700-AC.

    1. Open a web browser on the computer connected to the SBG6700-AC.
    2. Type 192.168.0.1 in the Address bar.
    3. Type admin in the Username field. Type password in the Password field. Click Login.
    4. Point to the Wireless Settings drop-down menu. Click 802.11 Radio
    5. Click the 2.4 GHz or 5 GHz tab. The steps are the same for both tabs.
    6. Select Enabled or Disabled from the Wireless Radio Enable drop-down list. This controls the Wi-Fi radio on the SBG6700-ac.
    7. In the Output Power drop-down menu, select the wireless network strength.
      • 100% Full Power
      • 75%
      • 50%
      • 25%
    8. In the 802.11 Mode drop-down menu, select the wireless networking mode.

      9. Select the 802.11 communication modes to best match the other devices on your network. You may see improved performance if you limit your network to only Wireless-N (802.11n) devices. Select additional modes for maximum compatibility with older equipment. Retain the default settings of b/g/n for 2.4GHz and a/n for 5GHz if you are unsure.

      • a/n/ac mode
      • n only mode
    9. In the Bandwidth drop-down menu, select the data transfer rate.

      10. The standard bandwidth for 2.4 GHz Wi-Fi networks is 20 MHz. Increasing the bandwidth can increase the amount of data that the network can carry. However, if the SBG6700-AC detects another Wi-Fi network using frequencies in the wider bandwidth, the SBG6700-AC will automatically reduce the bandwidth to the normal 20 MHz width.

      The ability to increase the bandwidth to 80 MHz is only available on the 5 GHz band.

      • 20 GHz (default)
      • 40 GHz
      • 80 GHz
    10. In the Channel drop-down menu, select a different channel number.
    11. Click Apply.

    Find Your MAC Address

    Your MAC address is the unique identifier assigned to network interfaces for communicating with your device. Wireless access points often use MAC addresses for access control. They only allow access for known devices with the correct passphrase.

    1. Click System Preferences
    2. Select Network.
    3. In the interfaces on the left, select Wi-Fi.
    4. Click the Hardware tab. You will see your computer's wireless MAC Address here.
    5. Click cancel to return to the Network menu.
    6. In the list of interfaces on the left, select Ethernet and then select Advanced.
    7. Click the Hardware tab to see the MAC Address of your ethernet card.

    Create a Guest Network

    A guest network is a great way for you to allow visitors to connect to your Wi-Fi network without giving out your password.

    A guest network allows you to grant access to your network to a select few friends, family or visitors without comprising your network security. After it's created, the guest network appears as a new, separate wireless network. A guest network also provides protection for your primary wireless network. Users of the guest network cannot see other devices on the main network.

    1. Open a web browser on the computer connected to the SBG6700-AC.
    2. Type 192.168.0.1 in the Address bar. Press enter.
    3. In the Username field, type admin.
    4. In the Password field, type password and then click Login.
    5. In the Wireless Settings drop down menu, click Guest Network Settings.
    6. Click the 2.4 GHz or 5 GHz tab. The steps are the same for both tabs
    7. In the Selected Guest Network drop down menu, select the guest network.
    8. Select Enabled or Disabled to turn the Guest Network on or off.
    9. You can change the guest network name or you can leave it as is.
    10. Select Guest.
    11. In the IP Address field, enter the IP Address for the SBG6700-AC.
    12. In the Lease Pool Starting IP Address field, enter the Starting IP Address.

      This is the first IP address that the SBG6700-AC will automatically assign to a device on your home network. If you wish to manually assign IP addresses to devices, set the starting local address above the range reserved for fixed addresses.

    13. In the Lease Pool Ending IP Address field, enter the Ending IP Address.
    14. In the Lease Time field, enter the lease time.

      This is the amount of time in seconds that an IP address is loaned to a device. When a device joins your network, the SBG6700-AC lends that device an IP address. When the device later leaves your network, the SBG6700-AC can then lend that IP address to a different device.

      In order to know which IP addresses are available, the SBG6700-AC lends devices an IP address for a specific period of time. This is known as the "lease time." When the lease time is reached, the IP address can be reused. If a device is staying connected to your network, the device will "renew" the lease, giving it more time to keep its assigned IP address.

    15. In the UPnP (Universal Plug and Play) Enable field, select Enabled.
    16. In the Firewall Enable field, select Enabled. This will ensure your guest network is secure.
    17. In the DHCPv6 Server field, select Enabled.
    18. In the Wireless Security drop down list, select one of the following wireless network security options for your wireless network.

      The security of your guest network is important. Without good security, intruders can not only use your network, but can intercept your Internet traffic and even gain access to your devices.

      The Guest Network of the SBG6700-AC offers two industry standard modes of Wi-Fi security: WPA2-PSK and WPA-PSK. WPA2-PSK offers the best security for your network and should be used unless you have devices that do not support WPA2-PSK. WPA2-PSK + WPA-PSK allows devices that do support WPA-PSK but not WPA2- PSK to connect with the network while still providing the added security of WPA2-PSK to the devices that do support it.

    Keeping the default username and password leaves your modem vulnerable. Someone could connect to your home network and make unauthorized changes in the gateway settings. You could even get locked out of your own wireless network.

    1. Open a web browser on the computer connected to the SBG6700-AC
    2. In the Username field, type admin.
    3. In the Password field, type password and then click Login.
    4. Click Manage Login Credentials on the home page.
    5. Click Change Username from the drop-down menu. You are now on the Change Username page.
    6. In the Enter Current Username field, enter admin.
    7. In the Enter Current Password field, enter password.
    8. In the Enter New Username field, enter a new username.
    9. Click Apply.
    10. Click Change Password in the drop-down menu. You are now on the Change Password page.
    11. In the Enter Username field, enter your username.
    12. In the Enter Current Password field, enter your current password.
    13. In the Enter New Password field, enter your current password.
    14. Click Apply.
